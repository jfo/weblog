Lastly. If the game is not to be played rigorously, according to the rules above mentioned, then moderate your desire of victory over your adversary, and be pleased with one over yourself.

You know, I've always really loved chess. I've never taken it particularly seriously, but ever since high school I've always had a friend or two around who is interested enough in it to play a few actual sit down, drag out, sweaty brow games with. I used to get walloped on yahoo chess too. I would think to myself "I'm pretty good so why am I getting beat so bad all the time?"

Reader, I was not really good.

By chess standards I was (and am, but we'll come back to that in a bit) truly bad.

First there was yahoo chess, then there was chess.com, and finally lichess, appealing as it does to my open source bones. I've been mediocre to bad on all of them at one point or another. Maybe a couple of times a year I'll go on a little kick, play a hundres or so blitz games, and if I'm lucky my rating will end a bit higher than it started that time around. Usually though, it might fluctuate a bit, maybe even go up some, only to crash tragically back down into the triple digits while I curse my own name in disgust and give up again until the next merry-go-round.

It was just some such downswing I was on a few weeks ago when I played a fateful game.

https://www.chess.com/analysis/game/live/5792137124

I had just put my 3 year old to bed, he insists that I stay in the hallway for a few minutes while he dozes off. This is fine, it's even a moment of enforced silence and peace for me, and it's an excellent time to get a good 3 min blitz game in. So I'm playing this game, half paying attention as I often did, keeping an ear out in case my kid needs me for something, and I'm really shitting the bed here, I mean it's bad. Dropping pieces like a real n00b, missing easy tactical wins, just a normal sub-par performance I had come to expect from myself. Then, it happens. My opponent, no doubt a true gentleman from jolly old england, chats me:

you suck

Now, this isn't the first time someone on an internet chess site has trash chatted me. Someone once threatened to kill my family after a rare checkmate! But something just snapped in me with this one. I started paying attention, and I crushed him. I was shaking, I was so mad.

After I forwent an easy checkmate to further humiliate him and ended up pawn forking his king and queen, I had my chance.

you suck

I said.

And that was that, that was the last rated game I played for the year on chess.com. I resolved at that moment, to stop fucking around, and take it seriously, to some extent at least.

Why be good at chess?

I mean, it's just a game.

https://www.chess.com/article/view/ben-franklin-and-chess https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Franklin/01-29-02-0608

What is my goal?

the goal, though this might shift, is 1500 on chess.com, 1700 on lichess.

Why? because 1500 is "where people start" according to Aman Hambleton. it's also eminently achievable. We're not talking about becoming a damn grandmaster here, we're just talking about becoming demonstrably better, dare I even say good in a general sense, at fucking chess, huh?

What's the plan?

The plan is, and this is key, no more blitz. Blitz is terrible for getting better! It's only good for playing blitz. Your pattern recognition doesn't have time to solidify when you're just playing game after game after game of garbage blitz gambits and whoever manages not to fuck up first wins. It's like trying to learn to play charlie parker heads at tempo. It's nuts! So no more blitz games.

No more distracted games.

I used to play at the bus stop. Bus comes? Resign. I used to play on the toilet. I used to play while watching TV. Why am I surprised I lost so much? I'm not applying my attention to the game, and again, mostly playing garbage blitz games. What did I expect to happen?

No more resigning.

I used to resign when the bus comes, sure, but I knew the bus was going to come, that's why I was at the bus stop. But I would resign if I got distracted, or if I just didn't want to play anymore, or if I lost a minor piece or felt like I screwed up or just in general felt like I wasn't going to win. This is the wrong attitude. At the level I and my opponents are at right now, anything can happen. Oh so I blundered a knight? Wait 5 more moves and there might just be a family fork waiting for me. Completely lost endgame? You will be shocked and amazed at your fellow 1200's inability to avoid stalemating you with a queen and a knight. Make them win. Make them work for it! Resignation is an admission of defeat in the face of utter hopelessness. It's a sign of faith in your opponent's ability, and respect for their knowledge and skill, to accurately play out the position. Do you have that faith? Is that respect due? Do you think a 1000 rated player will win 100% of the games that they are up a minor piece? Do you think you would? Make them play it out. Go for the draw, find a stalemate. I'll only resign from now on if I am both certain I am going to lose and that my opponent knows how to checkmate me. But even then, why not make them do it?

My first goal was to get my tactics rating to 1800. chess.com has tactics trainers. I though this step would take me until the end of the year, but actually it only took me a week to get it to 2000. Wow! Grandmaster here I come. Just kidding.

a rant on ratings

why tactics ratings are higher

Play ONE GAME A WEEK 15:10 rapid, as suggested by john bartholmew here (insert link). Make a big deal out of it. Make a cup of tea, turn off all the distractions, and play a full ass game as well as you possibly can. Then analyze it afterwards. If I assume I'll skip a week here or there, this is around 50 full games. That's actually a lot of games! I know that I'm capable of 1500 level play right now, on a good day, with focus. But I need to prove that, consistently, to actually achieve the rating.

Endgame study.

You would think I would start with some openings, right? Tactics and openings seem like a good place to begin. But the truth is I've lost way more games from a lack of endgame knowledge than from a lack of opening knowledge. Openings can get really intricate and deep, people analyze theory many levels deep to get insight into what positions are most beneficial for either side. But again, I'm playing people at my level, remember? The opening is mostly about development and avoiding gotchas. I already have an opening repertoire, and it's kind of bad but I've been playing it long enough to know how to get to the middlegame unscathed most always, which is enough for now. I'm going to add the sicilian against e4 and the giuoco piano for my own e4, I already play the indian against d4 so I should just properly study that one, it's pretty out there. If I have a d4/e4 openings for white and a preferred defense for same, well that covers 90 percent of games doesn't it? I think beginners and intermediates get scared off by openings because of how masters talk about them, but we're operating at a completely different, lower level. You don't have to study oodles of theory to "study openings", you just have to have a basic idea of the structure of the first 5 or so moves and move from there into the middlegame. I promise you your 1200 opponent will not have more knowledge than that, either. Just get through the opening.
