Let's take a bit of stock.

I've spent almost 2 months not playing any blitz chess. What have I done with that time?

I've done a ton of puzzles, usually up to 25 a day, and my chess.com puzzle rating has hovered around 2000 that time. My goal is to continue doing the puzzles there and maintain that score. Because of the vagaries of the chess.com puzzle rating system, and the fact that generally speaking, the higher the ratings go the more positional and combinational the solutions become, my goal initially was to stare at them til I get it and do them fast on the puzzle rush, instead,

I've started doing 3 chessable courses,

london for white scandi for black (and responding to d5 with my busted pirc repertoire for now) the endgame book.

Some observations: chessable is terrific, I really love it. It lets me drill this stuff with very little effort on my part. This is good, because my memory is shit but spaced repetition really works. It is slower going than I expected, but honestly, if I just work through these three courses this year, I predict my opening positions will improve dramatically.

Why did I pick these? I was originally going to start with endgames, but the truth is my openings are just so boring and stale to me. I've played shitty kings indian for years, and my white is always just a middle attack knights thing.

People make fun of the london, but it's got a lot going for it! It's flexible, can be opened or closed depending on how you play it, it's solid, and can be played against anything. I don't care if it's passe, it's the thing I chose. Also it's d4, which is a really good change for me.

Why the scandi? Similarly fashionable, and similarly maligned, but tbqh John Bartholemew is a really good teacher and it ticks some other boxes, too. It often castles kingside, so my positional knowledge from the kings indian is relevant sometimes, it's unexpected at lower levels, I think, and it's quite angular with sharp tactical opportunities.

My plan is to really commit to these openings for the year at least! Maybe next year I'll do giuoco piano and kings indian for real real.

I lied though, I've been sneaking some cheeky blitz games, first on anon but the strength was weirdly high? I don't know, could just be becasue I'm below average but wow really?

I had a weird week, week of inaugeration etc, and I just kind of spiraled a bit and played like 100 games on lichess. And SUCKED. I just kept losing, and I was getting really upset with myself. It feels so personal, you know? Here I am doing all these stupid puzzles and I'm worse than a few months ago?

So first of all, I wasn't worse. I was fucking tired as fuck, and fried mentally. The next day I played some games where I really focussed, and all those points came back and more. I even got my highest lichess rating ever! Which is dead average, and a good place to stop, but that's still pretty remarkable considering I'm playing new openings now.
